Getting Started

Today is the day; choose the laser that will fit your job and hobby.

Lasers are crystal clear, but the deatials can be fuzzy on which one is the best.

Diode Laser

Diode Lasers

  • Great for entry level
  • More affordabe than others/budget friendly
  • can engrave on plywood, cardboard, paper, cotton, leather, non-woven fabrics, some plastics, powder-coated and plated metals.
  • You can also etch on certain materials, such as: leather stainless steel and aluminum (best outcome when they are coasted)
  • Can cut hardwoods with multiple passes and depending on thickness.
  • Best for crafts, wood engraving, ornaments, images, logos,
  • Perfect for a home workshop or small design project.
  • Different brands: Comgrow Z1, xTool D1 Pro, Atomstack X7 Pro, Ortur LM2 Pro S2, Jinsoku LC-40

CO2

  • Powerful, easy to assemble, and comes in a variety of sizes and price points with different space requirements.
  • Often used in commercial settings
  • Will cost more than the entry-level diode models
  • They are much more powerful and still can cut a wide range of materials.
  • Wood, cardboard and paper, fabric and textiles, acrylic, leather, bamboo, rubber, anodized metal, glass, cement, stone, stainless steel
  • Will do 2D and 3D crafting and design projects.
  • Brands: Glowforge, OMTech, Flux, Boss Laser, Epilog Fusion Maker, Universal Laser Systems, Trotec Laser Cutter, Thunder Laser, Aeon Mira

Fiber Laser

Fiber Laser

  • More ideal for industrial cutting
  • Perfect for cutting, engraving, etching metals
  • Much more expensive than entry-level CO2 or diode models
  • Low maintenance and have very long commercia lifespan
  • Materials : gold, aluminum, steel, titanium, brass, copper, silver
